# Strong nontechnical explanation fixture

Think of ProseKernel like a prep sheet for an AI writer.

Before the agent writes, it looks up a few examples of the kind of piece you need: a launch email, an apology, a memo, or an empty state. It does not copy the wording. It copies the shape: where the proof appears, how the reader is named, and what the ending asks them to do.

Then it runs a scorecard. The scorecard checks for numbers, examples, weak openings, long sentences, and generic phrases like "frictionless productivity platform." If the draft sounds polished but says nothing, it fails.

The result is not magic prose but a slower, safer writing process that forces the agent to show its work. In a launch email, that means the claim appears after a concrete example; in an apology, the fix appears after the timeline.
