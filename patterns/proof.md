# Proof Patterns

## Strong proof

Strong proof connects evidence to claim.

Template:
> [Source/data/example] shows [specific fact], which means [specific implication].

## Weak proof

Weak proof gestures at credibility without doing work.

Examples:

- “featured in major outlets”
- “trusted by leaders”
- “research shows”
- “many experts agree”
- “widely recognized”

## Proof ladder

From weakest to strongest:

1. adjective
2. claim
3. named source
4. specific example
5. number / observed behavior
6. mechanism
7. repeated pattern across independent examples

## Humanprint rule

If a sentence asks for trust, it must pay with proof.
