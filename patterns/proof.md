# Proof Patterns

## Strong proof

Strong proof connects evidence to claim.

Template:
> [Source/data/example] shows [specific fact], which means [specific implication].

## Weak proof

Weak proof gestures at credibility without doing work.

Examples:

- “featured in major outlets”
- “trusted by leaders”
- “research shows”
- “many experts agree”
- “widely recognized”

## Proof ladder

From weakest to strongest:

1. adjective
2. claim
3. named source
4. specific example
5. number / observed behavior
6. mechanism
7. repeated pattern across independent examples

## Humanprint rule

If a sentence asks for trust, it must pay with proof.


## Specification-to-benefit proof

A technical spec becomes persuasive only when the reader can feel it.

Weak:
> 5GB storage.

Strong:
> 1,000 songs in your pocket.

Pattern:
> [technical capacity] → [human quantity] → [personal context]

## Constraint proof

Strategic writing becomes more believable when it names no-gos and tradeoffs.

Pattern:
> We will spend [appetite] because [problem]. We will not do [no-go], because [constraint].
