# Hook Patterns

## 1. Contradict the common belief

Template:
> Everyone says [common advice]. The real problem is [deeper truth].

Use when the reader already knows the topic but misunderstands the cause.

## 2. Specific pain + hidden cause

Template:
> You are not failing because of [obvious reason]. You are failing because [specific hidden mechanism].

Use for problem-aware readers.

## 3. Proof-first opener

Template:
> We tested [number] [users/examples]. [Specific surprising result].

Use when you have data.

## 4. Scene opener

Template:
> At [specific moment], [specific person] noticed [concrete anomaly].

Use when narrative is stronger than direct claim.

## 5. Compression hook

Template:
> [Short, memorable claim].

Then immediately unpack it.

## Bad hooks

- “In today’s fast-paced world...”
- “This post explores...”
- “Here are some insights...”
- “It is important to note...”


## 6. Portable distinction

Template:
> The conflict is not [surface issue]. It is [mode A] vs [mode B].

Source pattern: Paul Graham's maker/manager distinction.

Use when readers feel a pain but lack the language to explain it.

## 7. Human-benefit translation

Template:
> [Number] [desired outcomes] in [human context].

Source pattern: iPod “1,000 songs in your pocket.”

Use when a product has a technical spec that can be translated into lived value.
