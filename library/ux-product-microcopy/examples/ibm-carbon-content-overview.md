# IBM Carbon — Content Overview

## Source
- Author: IBM Carbon Design System
- URL: https://carbondesignsystem.com/guidelines/content/overview/
- Rights note: Metadata and original analysis only. Link to the source instead of copying it wholesale.

## Why this is good
Carbon treats content as part of system design, not decoration after UI decisions. It gives teams principles for clarity, usefulness, and consistency so product language can scale across surfaces without losing user focus.

## Craft moves
- Defines content as a design material with user-facing consequences.
- Connects voice and clarity to task completion.
- Provides system-level guidance rather than one-off copy tips.
- Supports consistency across large product ecosystems.
- Keeps the writer oriented toward user intent.

## Structure map
1. State the role of content in the product system.
2. Name principles that govern wording choices.
3. Explain how those principles affect user tasks.
4. Give teams repeatable standards for many surfaces.
5. Treat consistency as a usability feature.

## Excerpt or summary
Summary: IBM Carbon’s content guidance shows how enterprise UX writing becomes operational: clear principles, repeatable standards, and language tied to user tasks.

## Reusable pattern
For product content standards: “Content should help users [task]. Use [principle] when [surface/context]. Avoid [failure mode]. Check success by [user outcome].”

## Imitation prompt
Write product content guidelines in the Carbon spirit. Make the principles operational, connect words to user tasks, and avoid vague brand-voice advice.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Writing style rules that do not change product behavior.
- Optimizing for brand polish over task clarity.
- Letting every team invent different labels for the same action.
