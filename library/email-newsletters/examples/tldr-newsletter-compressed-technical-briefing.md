## Why this is good
TLDR works because it respects the technical reader’s time. It does not try to be a full article in every item; it gives enough context to decide what deserves a click. That makes compression a service, not a shortcut.

## Craft moves
- Groups items by reader-relevant domains.
- Summarizes why each link matters.
- Keeps item length disciplined.
- Lets expert readers choose depth.

## Structure map
1. Category blocks.
2. One-line orientation.
3. Concise summary and implication.
4. Link for depth.

## Excerpt or summary
Newsletter structure analysis only; no issue text stored.

## Reusable pattern
For expert curation, summarize just enough for triage and preserve links for depth.

## Imitation prompt
Write a technical briefing email that groups links by domain, gives concise implications, and avoids full-article bloat.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Summaries so vague the reader must click everything.
- Over-explaining news to expert readers.
- Mixing unrelated items without headings.
