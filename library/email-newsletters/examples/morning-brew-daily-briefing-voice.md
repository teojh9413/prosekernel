# Morning Brew daily briefing voice

## Source
- Author: Morning Brew
- URL: https://www.morningbrew.com/daily

## Why this is good
Morning Brew’s craft lesson is that curation needs voice, but voice cannot block utility. The newsletter uses a quick-read briefing format, topical segmentation, and light personality to make business news feel approachable.

## Craft moves
- Packages multiple topics into predictable sections.
- Uses voice to reduce dryness without hiding facts.
- Optimizes for morning skimming.
- Turns curation into a habit product.

## Structure map
1. Opening orientation.
2. Segmented brief items.
3. Context or implication.
4. Light recurring voice elements.

## Excerpt or summary
Newsletter product/format analysis only; no issue copy stored.

## Reusable pattern
For briefing emails, keep the facts scannable and let voice act as pacing, not decoration.

## Imitation prompt
Write a briefing email with clear sections, fast context, useful implications, and restrained personality.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Jokes that delay the point.
- Dense news summary with no reader implication.
- Random topic order that breaks skim behavior.
