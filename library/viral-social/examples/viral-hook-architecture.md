# Viral Hook Architecture

## Source
- Author: Humanprint synthesis
- URL: internal synthesis from existing writing wiki
- Rights note: Original synthesis.

## Why this is good
Viral writing has to earn attention fast without becoming empty clickbait. The best hooks create a knowledge gap, contradiction, or proof-backed surprise.

## Craft moves
- Contradicts common advice.
- Names a specific pain.
- Opens a loop.
- Signals proof or experience.
- Compresses the first sentence.

## Structure map
1. Hook with tension.
2. Context in one beat.
3. Core insight.
4. Example or proof.
5. Payoff / CTA.

## Excerpt or summary
Strong social writing is not shorter long-form writing. It is a pressure-tested sequence of tension, proof, and payoff.

## Reusable pattern
“Everyone says X. But after [proof/experience], I think Y. Here is the mechanism.”

## Imitation prompt
Write 20 hooks for this idea using contradiction, proof-first, pain-first, and scene-first openings. Pick the 3 with the strongest curiosity-to-substance ratio.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Clickbait with no payoff.
- Fake controversy.
- Thread structure that repeats the hook instead of developing it.
