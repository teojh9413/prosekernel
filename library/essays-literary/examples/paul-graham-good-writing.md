# Paul Graham: Good Writing

## Source
- Author: Paul Graham
- URL: https://paulgraham.com/articles.html
- Rights note: Metadata and analysis only. Link to original essays.

## Why this is good
Paul Graham’s essays show writing as thinking. The draft is not merely a container for ideas; it is the process that finds sharper ones.

## Craft moves
- Starts with a live question.
- Uses plain sentences for complex thought.
- Lets paragraphs turn as the thinking develops.
- Revises by sound and surprise.

## Structure map
1. Begin with a question or tension.
2. Explore examples.
3. Notice a distinction.
4. Push the implication.
5. End with a sharper thought than the opening.

## Excerpt or summary
ProseKernel takeaway: the writer is the first reader. If it sounds wrong, the thought is probably still wrong.

## Reusable pattern
Draft to discover, then edit to clarify.

## Imitation prompt
Turn this idea into a Paul Graham-style exploratory essay: begin with a real question, reason through examples, find a distinction, and end with a compressed insight.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Copying the cadence without the thinking.
- Using casual tone as a substitute for insight.
