# Zinsser: On Writing Well

## Source
- Author: William Zinsser
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_Writing_Well
- Rights note: Analysis only. Do not store book text.

## Why this is good
Zinsser treats simplicity as the result of hard thinking. His core standard is brutal: strip every sentence to its cleanest components.

## Craft moves
- Makes clutter the enemy.
- Connects clear writing to clear thinking.
- Insists on unity of pronoun, tense, mood, and content.
- Centers the transaction between one writer and one reader.

## Structure map
1. Know what you want to say.
2. Write naturally.
3. Cut clutter.
4. Preserve unity.
5. Read aloud.
6. Rewrite until the piece sounds inevitable.

## Excerpt or summary
ProseKernel takeaway: sound like a person, not someone trying to sound like a writer.

## Reusable pattern
For each paragraph, ask: what is the one job? If it has two jobs, split it. If it has none, cut it.

## Imitation prompt
Revise this draft with Zinsser’s simplicity standard. Remove clutter, preserve voice, and make each paragraph do one job.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Mistaking simplicity for generic phrasing.
- Removing the author’s personality along with clutter.
