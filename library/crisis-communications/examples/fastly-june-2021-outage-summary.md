## Why this is good
Fastly’s outage summary is a strong crisis example because it addresses impact and cause directly. The writing prioritizes reader trust: what happened, how broad it was, what triggered it, and what the company changed.

## Craft moves
- Acknowledges broad impact without euphemism.
- Separates trigger, underlying cause, and remediation.
- Uses concrete timing to reduce speculation.
- Explains prevention instead of relying on apology alone.

## Structure map
1. Impact and apology.
2. Timeline and trigger.
3. Technical cause in plain terms.
4. Mitigation and prevention.

## Reusable pattern
For infrastructure incidents, publish impact, timeline, cause, fix, and prevention in a sequence readers can audit.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Leading with brand reassurance before impact.
- Hiding cause behind passive language.
- Promising improvement without naming concrete changes.
