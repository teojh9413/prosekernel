## Why this is good
Atlassian’s outage update is useful because prolonged incidents require a different communication rhythm than short outages. The writing has to maintain trust across uncertainty by updating impact, recovery, cause, and customer next steps.

## Craft moves
- Communicates ongoing status instead of pretending closure.
- Names customer impact plainly.
- Explains recovery progress in stages.
- Balances accountability with operational detail.

## Structure map
1. Current customer impact.
2. What caused or contributed to the incident.
3. What has been restored.
4. What remains and what customers should expect.

## Excerpt or summary
Incident update analysis only; no source text mirrored.

## Reusable pattern
For extended incidents, use staged updates: current impact, known cause, recovery progress, remaining work, next communication.

## Imitation prompt
Write an ongoing incident update that gives customers current impact, recovery stage, known cause, next steps, and when they will hear more.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Writing as if the incident is over when customers still feel pain.
- Using internal project language customers cannot act on.
- Letting updates drift without a clear next checkpoint.
