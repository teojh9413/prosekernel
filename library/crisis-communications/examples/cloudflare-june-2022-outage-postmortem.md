## Why this is good
Cloudflare’s postmortem is useful because it treats trust as something rebuilt through detail. The strongest crisis writing does not hide behind apology language; it explains impact, sequence, cause, remediation, and future prevention.

## Craft moves
- States impact plainly.
- Provides a timeline readers can inspect.
- Explains cause without dumping blame.
- Separates immediate remediation from long-term prevention.

## Structure map
1. Impact summary.
2. Timeline.
3. Root cause.
4. Mitigation.
5. Prevention/lessons.

## Reusable pattern
For technical incidents, trust comes from inspectable specificity: impact, timeline, cause, remediation, prevention.

## Imitation prompt
Write an incident postmortem with a plain impact statement, timestamped timeline, root cause, fixes, and prevention measures.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Apologizing without explaining.
- Using passive voice to hide responsibility.
- Publishing vague prevention promises.
