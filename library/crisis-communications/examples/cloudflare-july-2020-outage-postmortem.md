## Why this is good
The postmortem earns trust by resisting vague apology language. It identifies impact, sequence, technical cause, and remediation. The craft move is accountable specificity: readers get enough detail to judge whether the team understands the failure.

## Craft moves
- Separates impact from root cause.
- Gives a timeline instead of a generic apology.
- Explains the technical failure without hiding behind jargon.
- States concrete remediation steps.
- Uses transparency as a trust-repair mechanism.

## Structure map
1. Acknowledge the incident and affected users.
2. Describe impact and duration.
3. Walk through timeline.
4. Explain root cause.
5. State remediation and prevention.

## Excerpt or summary
Summary: Cloudflare’s outage postmortem models infrastructure crisis writing: concrete impact, chronological account, cause, and prevention rather than reputation management.

## Reusable pattern
For outage communication: “At [time], [impact] affected [users/services]. Timeline: [events]. Root cause: [cause]. Fix: [actions]. Prevention: [system/process changes].”

## Imitation prompt
Write an incident postmortem in Cloudflare style. Be specific about impact, timeline, root cause, and prevention. Avoid minimizing or over-apologizing.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Saying “some users may have experienced issues” when impact is known.
- Offering remorse without operational detail.
- Conflating the fix with prevention.
