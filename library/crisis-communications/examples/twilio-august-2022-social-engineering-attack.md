## Why this is good
Twilio’s incident communication is a useful security example because the reader needs facts, scope, and action more than rhetoric. The piece shows how to discuss social engineering, customer impact, and response steps while avoiding vague reassurance.

## Craft moves
- Names the attack pattern directly.
- Separates confirmed facts from investigation context.
- Explains customer impact and response steps.
- Uses update posture rather than pretending instant completeness.

## Structure map
1. Incident identification.
2. Confirmed impact and investigation status.
3. Actions taken by the company.
4. Customer guidance and future updates.

Security incident communication analysis only; no source text mirrored.

## Reusable pattern
For security incidents, distinguish confirmed facts, likely mechanism, customer impact, actions taken, and what readers should do next.

## Imitation prompt
Write a security incident notice with confirmed facts, scope, response actions, customer steps, and update discipline.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Speculating beyond confirmed facts.
- Minimizing customer risk with vague reassurance.
- Leaving customers without clear action guidance.
