# Y Combinator — Requests for Startups

## Source
- Author: Y Combinator
- URL: https://www.ycombinator.com/rfs
- Rights note: Metadata and analysis only.

## Why this is good
RFS writing is strategic because it turns broad market change into a concrete invitation. It does not merely say “AI is big” or “healthcare is broken.” A good request names a specific opportunity, why now, and what kind of builder should care.

## Craft moves
- Converts trend into opportunity.
- Uses “why now” as proof of timing.
- Frames a problem in founder-actionable terms.
- Signals demand without pretending certainty.

## Structure map
1. Market/problem shift.
2. Why now.
3. Specific opportunity.
4. What a good attempt might look like.
5. Invitation to build.

## Excerpt or summary
Summary: Requests for Startups are public market theses written as calls for builders.

## Reusable pattern
“Because [change], [specific user/problem] is newly solvable. A strong company could [approach].”

## Imitation prompt
Write a YC RFS-style market thesis for this domain: name the change, why now, user pain, possible wedge, and what kind of founder should attack it.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Trend-chasing without a specific user.
- Writing a market map with no action vector.
