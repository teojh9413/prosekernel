# Buffett Shareholder Letters

## Source
- Author: Warren Buffett / Berkshire Hathaway
- URL: https://www.berkshirehathaway.com/letters/letters.html
- Rights note: Link and analysis only.

## Why this is good
Buffett’s letters make complex financial ideas feel understandable without making them simplistic. The voice builds trust because it is plainspoken, precise, and willing to explain mistakes.

## Craft moves
- Uses plain language for complex ideas.
- Explains incentives and accounting realities.
- Builds trust through candor.
- Uses analogy to make abstraction visible.

## Structure map
1. State result plainly.
2. Explain the mechanism.
3. Clarify what the metric does and does not mean.
4. Use analogy or example.
5. Name mistakes or risks directly.

## Excerpt or summary
Humanprint takeaway: intelligent writing does not need to sound academic. It needs to make hard things clear without hiding tradeoffs.

## Reusable pattern
When writing about complex performance, explain what happened, why it happened, what might be misleading, and what decision follows.

## Imitation prompt
Rewrite this technical/financial update in Buffett style: plain, candid, numerate, and allergic to promotional fog.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Folksy tone without substance.
- Oversimplifying risk.
- Hiding behind metrics.
