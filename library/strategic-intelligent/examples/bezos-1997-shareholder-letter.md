# Bezos 1997 Shareholder Letter

## Source
- Author: Jeff Bezos / Amazon
- URL: https://www.aboutamazon.com/news/company-news/amazon-1997-letter-to-shareholders
- Rights note: Link and analysis only.

## Why this is good
The letter is strategic writing because it names tradeoffs before they become controversial. It teaches readers how to judge future decisions: long-term market leadership over short-term accounting optics.

## Craft moves
- States the governing principle early.
- Makes tradeoffs explicit.
- Defines how the company should be evaluated.
- Turns strategy into a reusable decision filter.

## Structure map
1. Declare doctrine.
2. Explain why the doctrine matters.
3. Name the tradeoffs.
4. Show operating implications.
5. Invite the right kind of shareholder.

## Excerpt or summary
Core idea: optimize for long-term customer and market leadership, even when short-term financial appearance suffers.

## Reusable pattern
For strategic memos: “We will choose X over Y because Z. This means we will do A, avoid B, and measure C.”

## Imitation prompt
Write a founder-style strategic memo that states the principle, names the tradeoff, explains operating consequences, and filters for aligned readers.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Vague vision without tradeoffs.
- Strategy that sounds good but does not constrain action.
