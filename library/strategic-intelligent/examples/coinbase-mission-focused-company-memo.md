## Why this is good
The memo is useful as a writing example because it makes an uncomfortable boundary explicit. Whether one agrees with the stance or not, the craft move is clear: define the mission, name what falls outside it, explain the tradeoff, and accept consequences.

## Craft moves
- States a boundary instead of implying it.
- Acknowledges disagreement and cost.
- Connects policy to mission focus.
- Uses memo form to create organizational clarity.

## Structure map
1. Context.
2. Mission boundary.
3. Rationale.
4. Tradeoffs and consequences.
5. Next steps.

## Excerpt or summary
Public memo structure analysis only; no memo text stored.

## Reusable pattern
For contentious strategy, clarity requires naming both the chosen boundary and the cost of choosing it.

## Imitation prompt
Write a company memo that defines a strategic boundary, explains why it exists, acknowledges the tradeoff, and gives next steps.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Hiding the controversial part in euphemism.
- Pretending a tradeoff has no cost.
- Making a boundary without operational next steps.
