## Why this is good
The Day 1 letter is strategic writing because it makes a philosophy operational. It does not merely praise speed or customers; it identifies behaviors that preserve vitality and warns against process becoming a proxy for outcomes.

## Craft moves
- Names the core doctrine.
- Contrasts desired behavior with institutional drift.
- Turns philosophy into decision rules.
- Uses plain language for executive concepts.

## Structure map
1. Doctrine.
2. Threat to doctrine.
3. Operating principles.
4. Concrete behaviors.
5. Standard for future judgment.

## Reusable pattern
A strategic doctrine becomes useful when it names the enemy behavior and gives operators decision rules.

## Imitation prompt
Write a strategy memo that states a doctrine, names the decay it prevents, and translates it into operating rules.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Values without operating consequences.
- Strategy language that cannot guide decisions.
- Warning about risk without naming behaviors.
