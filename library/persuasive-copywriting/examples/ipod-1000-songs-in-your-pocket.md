# Apple iPod — 1,000 Songs in Your Pocket

## Source
- Author: Apple
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IPod_advertising
- Rights note: Short phrase referenced for analysis.

## Why this is good
The line translates storage capacity into human experience. It does not say gigabytes. It says what the capacity lets the buyer do.

## Craft moves
- Converts technical spec into lived benefit.
- Uses a number for concreteness.
- Adds physical imagination: “in your pocket.”
- Compresses the whole product story into one line.

## Structure map
1. Quantity.
2. Desired object.
3. Personal context.
4. Implied freedom.

## Excerpt or summary
Short referenced line: “1,000 songs in your pocket.”

## Reusable pattern
Translate technical capacity into the user’s desired world: “[number] [desired outcomes] in/without/inside [human context].”

## Imitation prompt
Rewrite this product’s technical specifications into 20 human-benefit lines. Every line must translate a feature into a concrete user outcome.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Naming a number the reader cannot feel.
- Using cleverness without a real product advantage.
