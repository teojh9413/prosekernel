# Jobs iPhone keynote three devices reveal

- Author: Steve Jobs / Apple

## Why this is good
The iPhone launch works as persuasion because it does not begin with specifications. It stages the reader/listener through familiar categories, lets expectations form, then collapses them into a single new object. The craft lesson is sequencing: make the audience understand the old boxes before showing why the new thing breaks them.

## Craft moves
- Frames the product through categories the audience already understands.
- Builds anticipation through repetition and controlled delay.
- Uses the reveal to make the product feel inevitable rather than merely feature-rich.
- Lets structure create drama before details appear.

## Structure map
1. Name the familiar alternatives.
2. Repeat the frame until the pattern is obvious.
3. Reveal that the alternatives are one integrated thing.
4. Only then explain capabilities and implications.

## Reusable pattern
Before listing features, create a simple category puzzle that the product resolves.

## Imitation prompt
Write a product launch section that frames three familiar alternatives, then reveals how one product unifies them without copying Apple phrasing.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Opening with a feature dump.
- Using suspense without a meaningful reveal.
- Claiming category creation before the reader understands the old category.
