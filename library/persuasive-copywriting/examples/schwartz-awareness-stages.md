# Schwartz Awareness Stages

## Source
- Author: Eugene Schwartz
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eugene_Schwartz
- Rights note: Framework summary and analysis only.

## Why this is good
The awareness ladder prevents a common writing mistake: saying the right thing to the wrong reader. A reader who does not know they have a problem needs education. A reader ready to buy needs friction removed. Mixing those messages kills persuasion.

## Craft moves
- Starts with reader state, not writer desire.
- Matches tone to awareness.
- Changes proof burden by stage.
- Makes persuasion diagnostic before rhetorical.

## Structure map
1. Diagnose awareness.
2. Choose message goal.
3. Choose formula.
4. Handle the next objection.
5. Ask only when the reader is ready.

## Excerpt or summary
Five stages: unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, most-aware.

## Reusable pattern
Before drafting, write: “The reader currently believes ____. After reading, they should believe ____.”

## Imitation prompt
Identify this reader’s awareness stage, then write the same message three ways: problem-aware, solution-aware, and most-aware.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Selling too early.
- Educating readers who already want the offer.
- Using urgency before desire exists.
