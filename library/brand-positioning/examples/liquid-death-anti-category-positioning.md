# Liquid Death anti category positioning

## Source
- Author: Liquid Death
- URL: https://liquiddeath.com/
- Rights note: Metadata and original craft analysis only; do not mirror full source text.

## Why this is good
Liquid Death shows how brand can create contrast where product differences are small. It borrows the codes of entertainment and rebellion, then attaches them to water so the category feels less polite and more identity-driven.

## Craft moves
- Chooses an unexpected category code.
- Turns commodity purchase into identity performance.
- Uses voice as differentiation, not garnish.
- Creates an enemy in boring, plastic-heavy beverage culture.

## Structure map
1. Reject the expected category tone.
2. Introduce the product through a distinctive attitude.
3. Attach the attitude to a simple behavior.
4. Invite the buyer into the joke and identity.

## Reusable pattern
For commodities, pick a sharp enemy and a borrowed cultural code that makes the product socially legible.

## Imitation prompt
Write positioning for a commodity product by rejecting the category default, naming the enemy, and making the purchase feel like identity.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Being weird without a strategic enemy.
- Using irreverence that hides what is being sold.
- Mistaking tone for actual positioning.
