# Linear purpose built product development positioning

## Source
- Author: Linear
- URL: https://linear.app/
- Rights note: Metadata and original craft analysis only; do not mirror full source text.

## Why this is good
Linear’s homepage positioning is effective because it narrows the audience and job. Instead of promising generic project management for everyone, it signals product-development specificity and a crafted workflow. Focus becomes a differentiator.

## Craft moves
- Names a specific workflow and audience.
- Uses product taste as credibility.
- Contrasts implicitly with bloated tools.
- Keeps the promise crisp and operational.

## Structure map
1. Specific audience/job.
2. Focused product promise.
3. Workflow proof.
4. Taste signal through restraint.

## Reusable pattern
A focused SaaS brand can win by saying exactly who it is built for and implying what it refuses to become.

## Imitation prompt
Write SaaS positioning that narrows the user, names the workflow, and uses focus as a proof point.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Claiming to serve every team.
- Using “all-in-one” when focus is the advantage.
- Confusing minimal copy with weak positioning.
