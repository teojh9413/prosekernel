# Apple: Think Different

## Source
- Author: Apple / TBWA\Chiat\Day
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Think_different
- Rights note: Analysis only. Do not store full ad script.

## Why this is good
The campaign positions the brand by celebrating the identity of its users. It sells a worldview before it sells a feature.

## Craft moves
- Makes the customer the hero.
- Uses identity, not specs.
- Creates an in-group around creative rebellion.
- Keeps the central phrase compressed and memorable.

## Structure map
1. Name the kind of person.
2. Celebrate their difference.
3. Connect difference to progress.
4. Transfer that identity to the brand.

## Excerpt or summary
Humanprint takeaway: brand writing becomes powerful when it gives the reader a self-image they want to inhabit.

## Reusable pattern
For brand manifestos: “We are for people who ____. The world calls them ____. We call them ____.”

## Imitation prompt
Write a brand manifesto that sells a worldview and customer identity before naming product features.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Empty inspiration without product connection.
- Grandiosity that the brand has not earned.
