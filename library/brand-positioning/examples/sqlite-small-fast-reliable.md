# SQLite: Small. Fast. Reliable. Choose any three.

## Source
- Author: SQLite
- URL: https://www.sqlite.org/
- Rights note: Short positioning line referenced for analysis.

## Why this is good
The line is memorable because it compresses positioning and objection handling into one sentence. It borrows the familiar “choose two” tradeoff frame and breaks it.

## Craft moves
- Uses extreme compression.
- Invokes a known category tradeoff.
- Reverses the tradeoff.
- Turns product virtues into a memorable claim.

## Structure map
1. Three desirable attributes.
2. Familiar constraint implied.
3. Constraint broken.
4. Product positioned as exception.

## Excerpt or summary
“Small. Fast. Reliable. Choose any three.”

## Reusable pattern
Name the three things the market assumes cannot coexist, then show your product as the exception.

## Imitation prompt
Create positioning lines that name a category tradeoff and break it with proof.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Using the form without actually breaking a real tradeoff.
- Compressing so much that the claim becomes unclear.
