# Tesla master plan public strategy narrative

## Source
- Author: Tesla / Elon Musk
- URL: https://www.tesla.com/blog/secret-tesla-motors-master-plan-just-between-you-and-me
- Rights note: Metadata and original craft analysis only; do not mirror full source text.

## Why this is good
The Tesla master plan is brand writing because it makes product sequencing feel like proof of a worldview. Expensive early products, mass-market ambition, and clean-energy mission become one story instead of disconnected business moves.

## Craft moves
- Turns roadmap order into moral logic.
- Explains premium entry as a path to broader access.
- Makes each product step prove the mission.
- Uses founder directness to reduce strategic ambiguity.

## Structure map
1. State the long-term mission.
2. Explain the initial wedge.
3. Connect early economics to future access.
4. Show the product sequence as strategy.

## Excerpt or summary
Metadata and original analysis only; no source text mirrored.

## Reusable pattern
When a roadmap looks counterintuitive, narrate the sequence as necessary steps toward a mission the audience already values.

## Imitation prompt
Write a founder strategy note that explains why the first product, next product, and eventual mass-market move belong to one mission.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Presenting roadmap as feature chronology.
- Using mission language that does not constrain product choices.
- Skipping the economic logic behind sequencing.
