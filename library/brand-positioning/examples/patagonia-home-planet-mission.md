# Patagonia home planet mission

## Source
- Author: Patagonia
- URL: https://www.patagonia.com/ownership/
- Rights note: Metadata and original craft analysis only; do not mirror full source text.

## Why this is good
Patagonia’s mission works because it is not decorative. It creates a public standard that product, ownership, and activism decisions can be judged against. The brand becomes memorable because the mission narrows what the company is allowed to be.

## Craft moves
- Makes the mission an operating constraint.
- Connects brand identity to sacrifice and decision rights.
- Creates external accountability.
- Lets values shape positioning instead of adding values after the fact.

## Structure map
1. Moral/mission frame.
2. Business implication.
3. Proof through decisions.
4. Invitation to judge future action by the standard.

## Reusable pattern
A serious mission statement should constrain behavior, not merely decorate the About page.

## Imitation prompt
Write brand positioning that states a mission, shows what it forces the company to do, and names what it refuses.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Values language with no operational cost.
- Treating mission as campaign copy.
- Making the brand sound virtuous without proof.
