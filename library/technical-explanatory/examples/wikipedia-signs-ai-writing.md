# Wikipedia: Signs of AI Writing

## Source
- Author: Wikipedia contributors
- URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wikipedia:Signs_of_AI_writing

## Why this is good
This is not a “how to write well” page. It is more useful: a field guide to how AI writing fails. It names the surface tells and points toward deeper causes: generic claims, fake significance, superficial analysis, weak attribution, and platform-mismatched formatting.

## Craft moves
- Names failure modes precisely: legacy inflation, vague attribution, superficial analysis.
- Separates symptoms from root problems.
- Uses real examples to show how generic importance replaces specific fact.
- Warns against relying on detectors or single tells.

## Structure map
1. Caveats: detection is uncertain.
2. Core theory: LLMs regress toward statistical average.
3. Content tells: inflated significance, notability signaling, superficial analysis.
4. Language/style tells: AI vocabulary, formulaic patterns, formatting artifacts.
5. Citation/markup tells: broken references, fake precision.
6. Human signs: explainable editorial choices.

## Excerpt or summary
Core Humanprint takeaway: AI writing often makes the subject sound more important while making the actual facts less specific.

## Reusable pattern
When editing a draft, search for places where specificity faded and importance inflated. Replace broad significance with concrete mechanism.

## Imitation prompt
Audit this draft for Wikipedia-style AI-writing tells: legacy inflation, superficial analysis, vague attribution, formulaic formatting, and fake proof. For each problem, explain the deeper writing failure and rewrite with concrete evidence.

## Anti-patterns to avoid
- Treating tells as proof of AI authorship.
- Only replacing banned phrases while leaving generic thought intact.
- Using the page as a detector instead of a quality guide.
