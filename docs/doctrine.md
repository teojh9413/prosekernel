# ProseKernel Doctrine

## Prime directive

Write like a specific human speaking to one specific reader about one specific thing that matters.

If the draft could appear on any generic SaaS blog, it fails.

## The CLEAR foundation

- **Cut ruthlessly** — remove anything that does no work.
- **Lead with the point** — first sentence carries the claim, tension, scene, or promise.
- **Eliminate dead language** — no clichés, filler, corporate fog, or AI cadence.
- **Active construction** — name who did what.
- **Read aloud** — if the mouth stumbles, the mind stumbles.

## ProseKernel laws

### 1. Specificity beats significance

Bad AI writing inflates importance when it lacks detail.

Weak:
> This pivotal innovation marks a significant shift in the evolving landscape.

Strong:
> The form lost 18 of 42 test users at the company-size field.

### 2. Proof beats praise

Do not say “world-class,” “revolutionary,” or “best.” Show the evidence that would make a reader conclude it.

### 3. Structure beats decoration

A table, boldface, em dash, or list does not make weak thinking strong. Formatting should reveal structure, not fake it.

### 4. Voice is judgment, not personality sprinkles

Human voice comes from choices: what to include, what to cut, what to emphasize, what to risk saying.

### 5. Match the reader’s awareness

Use Schwartz’s awareness ladder:

- Unaware: reveal the problem.
- Problem-aware: agitate and clarify the cost.
- Solution-aware: differentiate the mechanism.
- Product-aware: prove and handle objections.
- Most-aware: remove friction and ask directly.

### 6. Do structure transfer, not phrase transfer

Study examples for moves:

- how the opening creates tension
- how proof enters
- how the argument turns
- how the close lands

Never copy the surface language.

## What good writing should do

A good piece should make the reader think at least one of these:

- “That is exactly my problem.”
- “I had not thought of it that way.”
- “This person has done the work.”
- “I want to steal that line.”
- “I should send this to someone.”
