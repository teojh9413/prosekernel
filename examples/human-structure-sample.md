# Why I am writing this now

This sample draft shows the more situated structure ProseKernel expects from editorial architecture diagnostics.

Your payments business already has three assets most AI vendors would have to buy or borrow: merchant trust, transaction context, and a reason to be inside the workflow every week. That makes this a better moment to discuss AI than a generic platform pitch suggests.

# What I noticed in the current business

The obvious move is support automation. I would not start there. It may reduce cost, but it does not change the company's story or create a new reason for merchants to come back between payment events.

# The part I would not build first

I would avoid a broad AI transformation program. It asks the organization to believe too much before one commercial wedge has proven itself.

# The commercial path worth testing

The sharper path is a merchant cashflow layer: invoice follow-up, reconciliation, and financing signals built around data the company already sees. The point is not to turn payments into software theater. It is to test whether the company can become useful before and after the transaction.

# What the first conversation should decide

The useful first meeting is simple: pick one merchant segment, decide whether the cashflow wedge is credible, and define a 30 day discovery sprint that would prove or kill it.
